Newton's First Law of Motion

Newton's first law of motion is as follows:

A body remains at rest, or in motion at a constant speed in a straight line, unless acted upon by a force.

Or in other words, if nothing happens to an object (no force is applied to it), then it will maintain it's velocity. Now this law we might find a little more surprising. Indeed, generally for life on earth, if we want to go somewhere we have to continually put it effort. Whether it's effort from our body to walk or run, or fuel in the engine of a car being burnt, the action is continuous. We can't just move a little bit and expect for that energy to keep us moving.

Perhaps this is why the famous Greek philosopher Aristotle came up with his own law (which turned out to be false), that an object's natural state is to be at rest and if you stop trying to move it, it will come back to rest. This would be a more familiar framework, but is in fact, overlooking a great deal of things that happen in all of our interactions on Earth.

This brings us on to the concept of Drag and Friction.
