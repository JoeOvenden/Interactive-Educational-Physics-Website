Buoyancy

Buoyancy, also known as upthrust, is an upward force exerted by a fluid upon a partially of fully submerged object and is the force that is responsible for objects floating. The magntitude of this force is equal to the mass of fluid that the object is displacing multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity.

Suppose there was a sphere of radius, r and density, d, which is fully submerged in a fluid of density D, in a gravitational field of strength, g. The force due to buoyancy will be:

mass of fluid displaced * acceleration due to gravity =

volume of fluid displaced * fluid density * acceleration due to gravity =

volume of the sphere * fluid density * acceleration due to gravity =

4⁄3πr3Dg

The force due to gravity will be:

mass * -acceleration due to gravity =

volume of sphere * sphere density * -acceleration due to gravity =

-4⁄3πr3dg

At this point hopefully you will notice that the two equations look very similar. Let's see what we get if we now try to resolve the two forces.

Resultant force =

4⁄3πr3Dg - 4⁄3πr3dg =

(4⁄3πr3g)(D - d)

Looking at the left hand bracket we see that g, r^3, pi and 4/3 are all positive, meaning the left hand bracket is a positive number. Looking at the right hand bracket we see that it is positive when D > d, it is 0 when D = d and it is negative when D < d. A positive resultant force means the sphere will be accelerated upwards, a negative resultant force means the sphere will be accelerated downwards, and force of 0 means there will be no acceleration.

The result of this is that an object will float to the top in a fluid when it's density is lower than the fluid density, it will sink when it's density is higher than the fluid density, and it won't move when it's density is equal to the fluid density.
