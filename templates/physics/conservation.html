Conservation of Energy

Energy comes in various different forms, for example; we can have energy in the form of movement, called kinetic energy; energy in the form of heat; potential energy, energy that is "stored" in an object by gravity or some other means (imagine a book on a shelf, if we push the book off the shelf, it will gain kinetic energy. We say that the potential energy is converted to kinetic energy).

The law of conservation of energy is that:

Energy is always conserved, meaning it is never destroyed. Energy can only ever be converted from one form to another.

So for instance say we take some wood and burn it. There is chemical energy stored in the wood, when we burn it we convert that energy mostly into heat energy, but some into light too. That heat energy will then spread out and heat up the air and all the surroundings.

Another example: we drop a heavy ball onto the ground. The ball starts off with gravitational potential energy which gets converted into kinetic energy. Then when the ball hits the ground, it sends vibrations through the ground which is also kinetic energy. Instead of having a ball that is moving fast, instead we now have a ball that is still and the ground that is vibrating ever so slightly more than it was before.

Conservation of Momentum

The momentum of an object is defined as such:

Momentum = mass x velocity

Now it turns out momentum is also conserved, meaning it can never be lost, it can only be transferred to and from different objects.

Let's look at an example:

Imagine a rocket in space not moving. It currently has net momentum of 0 in all directions. Say the pilot now wants to move the rocket in a direction s. Well conservation of momentum has to be conserved and so what happens is that for the rocket to gain velocity , gas is shot out in the direction opposite to s very fast.

The gas that is shot out has negative momentum in the direction s, since it is travelling in the opposite direction and so through conservation of momentum, the rocket gains positive momentum in th direction s of the same magnitude as that of the gas.

The result is that the net momentum is still 0, but instead we now have gas travelling in one direction and the rocket travelling in the other.
