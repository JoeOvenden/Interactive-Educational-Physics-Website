Understanding the laws of motion

There are many laws that govern our world. Often when we talk about laws we are referring to man-made laws, constructed and enforced by governments to control certain aspects of a society. However unlike man-made laws, there are laws that are not socially constructed. They "existed" before us and will be here after we're gone, and what's more, they can't be broken! These are the laws of nature.

Now there are many laws of nature to be explored, understood and utilised, however here we will conscern ourselves with the basics and specifically ones that are important for the understanding of space, namely, the laws of motion.

Gravity

Gravity is the law of attraction of mass. That is, that everything with mass is attracting everything else with mass. The more mass, the stronger the attraction. That is why we as humans in the presence of the Earth's gravity are pulled towards the Earth, and if we were to say, throw a ball up into the air, it will come down. What is less familiar to us however is the situation of not being in the presence of a strong gravitation field, like for instance, in most of space. We'll come back to gravity in the section on black holes but for now, this brings us to another law of nature.
